Why I Started Writing

March 2025 5 min read

I spend most of my working hours making sense of data for other people. Turning a messy CSV into a dashboard someone can glance at and immediately understand. Building a model that predicts which customers are about to leave, so a salesperson can do something about it before they do. Translating numbers into a story the product team can act on. That's the job.

Writing, I've discovered, is how I make sense of things for myself.

There's a specific kind of thinking that only happens when you're trying to explain something in prose. It's different from the thinking you do when you're building a model or cleaning a dataset. When I write, I find the edges of what I actually understand. The vague sense that I know something gets tested — and often, the test reveals I don't quite know it as well as I thought.

The problem with just consuming

I read a lot. Papers, blog posts, books about systems and statistics and software. For a long time I thought that reading was enough — that ideas absorbed were ideas owned. But there's a difference between being able to nod along to something and being able to explain it from scratch.

Writing forces the second thing. You can't hide behind "roughly, it works like this" when you're trying to put a sentence on the page. Either you know it or you don't. And if you don't, writing shows you exactly where the gap is.

"If you can't explain it simply, you don't understand it well enough." The cliché is a cliché because it keeps being true.

What I want this to be

I'm not starting this blog to build an audience or establish a brand or any of that. I'm starting it because I have thoughts I want to work through, and I want a record of them.

Some posts will be technical — things I learned about data science, tools I found useful, analyses that surprised me. Some will be more personal — ideas about work and learning and what it means to be curious about too many things at once.

I have no posting schedule. No editorial strategy. Just a place to write when I have something to say.

If you're reading this and any of it resonates, feel free to reach out. I'd like that.
